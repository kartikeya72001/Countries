The City

Delhi, India’s capital territory, is a massive metropolitan area in the country’s north. In Old Delhi, a neighborhood dating to the 1600s, stands the imposing Mughal-era Red Fort, a symbol of India, and the sprawling Jama Masjid mosque, whose courtyard accommodates 25,000 people.

The Food

What food is Delhi famous for? A. Paranthas, chaat, butter chicken, kebabs, chole bhature, biryani, rolls, samosa, and gol gappa are some of the best food that you must try in Delhi.

The People

The people of Delhi are referred to as Delhiites or Dilliwalas. The city is referenced in various idioms of the Northern Indo-Aryan languages. The People of Delhi are a mixed bag of Indians, Pakistanis and Immigrants and hence it greatly influences the kind of lifestyle and culture that they follow.

How To Reach ?

Delhi is well connected by air, railways and roads with all major cities across India. For a comfortable journey you can take a flight to Delhi. Indira Gandhi International Airport is just 20 km away from the city center. The railway stations in Delhi are all well connected with different parts of the city.
